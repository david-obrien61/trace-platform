Shoal Creek Vitex (Vitex agnus-castus ‘Shoal Creek’)

Shoal Creek Vitex (Vitex agnus-castus ‘Shoal Creek’)  is a deciduous shrub or small tree prized for its fragrant blue-violet flowers and attractive foliage. It’s a popular selection for gardens due to its vibrant blooms, which attract butterflies, bees, and hummingbirds. It’s also relatively low-maintenance, drought-tolerant, and can be used in various landscape settings. 

  • Growth Habit: Can be grown as a large shrub or trained into a small, multi-trunked tree 
  • Mature Size: Typically reaches a height of 8 to 15 feet and a width of 12 to 15 feet
  • Foliage: Compound, palmate leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets, grayish-green in color, and fragrant
  • Flowers: Produces showy, fragrant, blue-violet flower spikes (racemes) in mid-summer
  • Hardiness: Generally hardy in USDA zones 6-9, but may experience dieback in colder zones
  • Sunlight: Thrives in full sun
