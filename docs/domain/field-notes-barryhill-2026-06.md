## CONFIRMED FINDINGS

### 1. Buyer-per-category structure — inventory cognition is distributed across humans (T)
Department leads / buyers, **one per category** (perennial, houseplant, tropical — "all pretty categorized"). Key quote:
> "A lot of our buyers are on the floor, working with customers, so they know without having to walk over and check a table."

**The per-category buyer is the unit of inventory cognition** — it lives partitioned across people's heads, by category. → **DESIGN NOTE:** may affect how roles/permissions and the walk-and-count tool are scoped (count + reconcile may be naturally per-category, owned by that category's buyer). Confirms the buyer-per-category note flagged in the master bank (#64). Relates to [ontology §3](ontology.md#3-category) (category taxonomy) and the role/permission model.

### 2. No official inventory system — at a 4.5★ full-service garden center (T)
> "Clipboard with the order sheet and just visually check… we don't have any sort of official inventory system."

Excel used "in terms of tracking." → **Firms the ~88% no-system / Structure-Last thesis ([[OP-10]]) with field evidence**: even a sophisticated, well-reviewed operation runs on clipboards + heads, not a system. Feeds [ontology §7](ontology.md) and the [no-system grower story](../../user_stories.md) (asset-inventory-pmi arc).

### 3. POS is sales-only — does not decrement inventory (T)
> "Our POS doesn't work like that, it's an old-school register, sales-department sales… there's no way to track individual variety." · "The QR is not even set up. Super basic, structurally as it comes."

**This is the reconciliation hole, in the grower's own words** — sales happen but stock never decrements per-variety, so on-hand drifts from reality. Confirms exactly why Cultivar's **walk-and-count → reconcile** loop (cap 2.3 / arc-8) fills a real gap. Feeds [ontology §3](ontology.md#3-category) + §7.

### 4. Budget-from-last-year (T)
> "We take last year's budget, add another 5–10%."

(The cold-start-when-new framing was D's.) Feeds [ontology §7](ontology.md) business mechanics — how a grower sets buying volume.

### 5. Seasonal reconciliation rhythm — PER CATEGORY (D→T) — **ANSWERS THE OPEN RESEARCH ITEM**
This **confirms and closes** the reconciliation-rhythm question the master bank (#64) flagged as "pending clean transcript." The model is **per-category**, co-constructed and field-validated:

- **Seasonal / veg = fast clock, markdown-or-lose** — "gotta be in the ground by end of June… put it on sale, almost free, gonna die, at least get our money back." *(D framed, T confirmed.)*
- **Trees = slow clock, carry over, APPRECIATE** — "if we don't sell it this year we add fertilizer, it grows, may repot, still OK." *(T.)*
- **Tropicals = inverse clock, get out before winter** — "get out as quick as possible, tropical going into wintertime." *(T added.)*

→ Feeds [ontology §3](ontology.md#3-category) (the seasonal-vs-specimen split that drives reconciliation rhythm) + §6 lifecycle. The ontology §3 note is now flipped from open-research to **CONFIRMED (field-validated, per-category)**.

### 6. Pot-size vernacular — genuine grower-sourced naming variance (T)
> "The square ones are 4-inch containers… if it's annuals usually they call a **QUART**, if it's a houseplant they'll call it a quart or 4-inch or 6-inch pot just depending; the bigger ones are by the **gallon**."

**The real field-sourced example of one canonical size having many spoken names** — and the name varies by *what's in the pot* and *who's speaking*. This is legitimate size-naming-variance evidence, **distinct from the Happy-hose example** (which was D's, see caveat). Feeds [ontology §1](ontology.md#1-size) (size systems) + §2 (naming) — field evidence that size terms are spoken loosely and must normalize (ledger #62 `normalizeSize`), and that a grower's size list is their own subset, never a global enum.

### 7. Lean-inventory / anti-overextension philosophy (T) — **field validation of the suggest-less doctrine**
The sharpest strategic finding. Trinten's store deliberately runs **LEAN**:
> "A lot of garden centers super overextend themselves… reps from growers come in, see your space, and buy to completely fill the nursery… that's consignment, like H-E-B big box — they pack as much inventory as they can."

His store: minimal inventory, high specialization (African violets from just 3 specialty growers), "get in and out without overextending."

→ **This validates the margin-aware "suggest buying LESS, not more" doctrine from a real, savvy practitioner.** Overextension is seen as the *industry's disease*; a sharp small operator already thinks this way — it is not a niche idea. High-value for the [MASTER_BRIEF](../../MASTER_BRIEF.md) cost/margin thesis and the suggestion engine ([[D-25]] suggest-buy-less, [[OP-9]] Regina Principle). Feeds [ontology §7](ontology.md) (business mechanics) + [ontology §4](ontology.md#4-sourcing) (the order grammar / how much to buy).

### 8. Inventory/grocery crossover (T)
> "Garden center inventory has a big crossover with grocery store inventory management — it's one of their systems we ended up implementing in horticulture."

→ **Nursery inventory borrows from grocery** (perishable, seasonal, fast-turn). Feeds [ontology §7](ontology.md) (business mechanics) — a precedent for the patterns the platform should speak.
